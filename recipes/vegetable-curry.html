Vegetable Curry!

Description

This recipe takes inspiration from Japanese style curry and rice! Japanese curry is historically based somewhat on indian curry brought by the British during the 1800s. This dish contains a ton of delicious flavor from the spices, and the vegetables and soy curls provide an amazing meal! The best part of this recipe is that it is relatively quick and makes a lot of Curry which is also fairly light on calories but high in nutrition!

Ingredients

Vegetables

  • 4-6 Yukon Gold Potatoes
  • 2 Onions
  • 3 Carrots
  • 1/2 lb soy curls rehydrated (1/4 lb dry)
  • 6 oz Mushrooms
  • 4 Cloves Garlic
  • 4 tsp Grated Ginger

Misc

  • 1 box Curry Roux (~120 grams)
  • 1 Onion, Caramelized
  • 2 tbsp Worcestershire Sauce
  • 2 tbsp Ketchup
  • 2 tbsp Soy Sauce
  • 2 tsp Cocoa Powder
  • 2 tsp Instant Coffee
  • 5 cups Water

Steps

  1. Over medium high heat, add onions and fry for 1 minute. Add in garlic and ginger and fry for another minute. Add in the mushrooms, carrots, potatoes and soy curls, and cook for 5 minutes.
  2. Add water. Bring to a boil and then reduce heat to a low and simmer for 15 minutes or until you can pierce a toothpick through it.
  3. Turn off the heat. Chop up the roux into small pieces (makes it easier to dissolve). Add the chopped roux to a soup ladle and dissolve it well into the curry. Stir in misc ingredients.
  4. Turn off heat and simmer to thicken. About 5 minutes.
  5. Serve over rice!
